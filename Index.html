A Tribute to

VIRAT KOHLI

"Virat Kohli is an Indian international cricketer and former captain of the Indian national team who plays as a right-handed batsman for Royal Challengers Bangalore in the IPL and for Delhi in Indian domestic cricket"

Virat Kohli is a famous Indian cricketer. He was a former captain of the Indian team and had captained his team in 213 international matches across all formats. In the Indian Premier League, Virat was the captain of the Royal Challengers Bangalore franchise for nine consecutive seasons from 2013 to 2021.

Born on 5 November 1988, Virat Kohli is known for his aggressive captaincy style and his fearless attitude. He is considered to be one of the most incredible batters and captains in the world.

Virat Kohli has an impeccable captaincy record in Test cricket. He has captained India in 68 Test matches and won 40 of them, becoming the most successful Indian Test captain.

Most double centuries by an Indian in Test cricket

In 2019, Kohli surpassed Sachin Tendulkar and Virendra Sehwag to smash most double centuries by an Indian in test cricket. Kohli has seven centuries in the long format of the game, while Sachin and Sehwag have six double centuries each.

Most runs in a single IPL season – 973

Kohli scored 973 runs in the ninth edition of the Indian Premier League to guide his team to the finals of the tournament. The season saw him score as many as four centuries — the most by a player in a single season

Most runs in a calendar year/series

In 2017, Kohli slammed 11 centuries across all formats of the game and amassed 3000 runs. As a captain, he led India to new heights, winning all the series at home. Most ODI runs in a calendar year by an Indian cricketer – 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016

Virat Kohli’s ODI match records

As of January 2023, Kohli is the fifth-highest run-scorer (12809) in ODI matches. His batting average stands at 58.23. He has scored 46 ODI centuries and is only three centuries behind Sachin Tendulkar’s record of 49, has hit 1,204 fours and made 141 catches in ODIs.

Greatest Test Captain

Virat Kohli has an impeccable captaincy record in Test cricket. He has captained India in 68 Test matches and won 40 of them, becoming the most successful Indian Test captain.

Virat has been the captain for 24 Test series, won 18 of them, and has not lost a single series at home. Under his captaincy, India defeated Australia in a Test series for the first time in Australia.

He also led India into the finals of the World Test Championship. In his tenure of seven years as the Test captain, Kohli holds the record for scoring the most number of double centuries and for being the highest run-getter as an Indian Test captain.
